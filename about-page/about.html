Jane         Yao

I am an 18 year old student currently studying Computer Science at the University of Waterloo. I was born and raised in Ottawa, Ontario and a proud second generation Canadian, with parents who immigrated from China. Building websites in a grade 10 computer science course, I discovered my interest in combining design and computation. Awwwards and MetaLab have inspired me to work more in depth with regards to visual design.

Aside from photography and programming, I keep myself busy with sports, music, art, and volunteering.

Music


Aside from jamming out to the Jonas Brothers and Cardi B, I have been playing music for over 10 years. I have completed piano levels 1-10, earning RCM certificates for leels 5 and 8, as well as music theory knowledge from basic rudiments to basic harmony and music history. In addition to the piano, I have played the tenor saxaphone as a part of my school's concert band, and taught myself the ukulele and vocals. Music has been instrumental throughout my life. Harmonizing or sight reading msic taught me how to think creatively.

Although I am not a natural performer, performing my pieces has taught me the importance of practicing an art to perfect it, how to think on your feet when you make a mistake, and most of all how to trust myself.


Visual Arts


In addition to photography, my interest in visual arts include sketching, UI/UX design, multimedia art, and watercolour. As a self-taught artist, it is difficult to build up a high level of technical skill. However, years of Pinterest-ing, using helpful software like Adobe Photoshop/AfterEffects, and admiring various forms of graphic design I see daily, I have learned to actualize my artistic ideas.

I have worked in acryllic and watercolour paint, pen, and in multimedia to produce my pieces. I hope to use my creativity and artistic ability in my work as a computer science student.

Combining computation/problem-solving with design and art is one of the reasons why I enjoy building websites. In the future I hope to expand both my technical knowledge in front and back end development to be a successful full stack developer.
